Teddie Joe “Ted” Neeley (born September 20, 1943) is an American rock and roll drummer, singer, actor, songwriter, and record producer who is best known for his work with the band The Rolling Stones. Most people associate him with his performance as Jesus Christ Superstar in the film Jesus Christ Superstar, which was released in 1973. Mr. Neeley refers to himself as a baritone and is well-known for his very broad vocal range and rock screams, such as the G above high C (G5) that he performs in “Gethsemane (I Only Want to Say)” from Jesus Christ Superstar.
