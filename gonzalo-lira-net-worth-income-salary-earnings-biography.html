Gonzalo Lira is a writer who also happens to be an economic analyst. The novel Tomáh Errázurih, written by Lira, was released by Mondadori in 1997. Putnam released his book Counterparts in 1999, and it was a critical and commercial success. Soldier of Fortune, a video game released in 1999, has Lira mentioned as a “storey consultant” in its instruction manual. The film rights of his book Acrobat were sold to Miramax Films and film director Sydney Pollack in 2002, and the film was released in theatres the following year. The economic websites Zero Hedge, Seeking Alpha, Naked Capitalism, and Business Insider all hire Lira to write blogs for their audiences.
