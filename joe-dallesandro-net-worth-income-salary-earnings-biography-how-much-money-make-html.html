Joe Dallesandro III (born December 31, 1948), often known as Joe Dallesandro, is an American actor and pop culture icon who rose to prominence during the Andy Warhol era. Despite the fact that he never achieved mainstream success as a film star, Dallesandro is widely regarded as the most renowned male sex symbol of American underground cinema throughout the twentieth century, as well as a sex symbol of homosexual subculture. During the filming of Flesh, Dallesandro played the role of a juvenile street hustler. He was elevated to stardom in 1970 when Rolling Stone magazine named his second starring vehicle, Trash, as “Best Film of the Year,” establishing him as a celebrity of the 1970s youth culture, sexual revolution, and subcultural New York art collective movements.
