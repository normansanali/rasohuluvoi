Julien Baker bio, age, career, net worth, family & more-American Indie rock singer and guitarist, Julien Rose Baker was born on September 29, 1995.

Born in Germantown, Tennessee, Baker was exposed to music early on by performing at her church as she was raised in a devoted Baptist family.

She became interested in alternative music after watching Green Day on television and began listening to groups like My Chemical Romance and Death Cab for Cutie.

Later, she was enthralled by the punk, hardcore, metalcore, and screamo scenes. She has listed mewithoutYou, Underoath, The Chariot, Norma Jean, and Whitechapel as some of her favorite bands.

As a young girl, she battled substance misuse but found support in Memphis’ house show scene and was motivated by the straight-edge punk subculture.

In 2010, while still in high school, Baker co-founded the band the Star Killers, which later changed its name to Forrister.

Baker attended Arlington High School before transferring to Middle Tennessee State University, where she worked in the A/V department and pursued secondary education and literature after initially studying audio engineering.

After Sprained Ankle was released, she eventually dropped out of school to tour full-time but then came back to campus in the fall of 2019 to finish her literature degree.

Julien Baker career

Baker started writing songs on her own during her first year at MTSU, frequently using the university’s late-night practice spaces.

She stated that she never imagined the EP would be heard by a large audience; she posted it on Bandcamp so that her friends could hear it.

She penned what would become Sprained Ankle in her dorm room and recorded it on free studio time that a friend of hers had obtained from an internship.

However, 6131 Records found it and acquired it, turning it into the studio album Sprained Ankle, which was released in October 2015.

Many year-end lists for 2015 had Sprained Ankle at the top, and because of its popularity, it was highlighted in The New Yorker and The New York Times. Critics have praised the book as “heartbreaking,” “hypnotic,” and “striking.”

She joined Matador Records in 2017 and put out a 7-inch single with the songs “Distant Solar Systems” and “Funeral Pyre” (formerly known as “Sad Song 11”).

Turn Out the Lights, her second album, was created at Ardent Studios in Memphis with engineer and producer Calvin Lauber and released on October 27, 2017, to much fanfare.

Numerous musicians, such as Death Cab for Cutie, Conor Oberst, Paramore and Hayley Williams, The National, The Decemberists, Belle & Sebastian, Frightened Rabbit, The Front Bottoms, Touche Amore, Manchester Orchestra, and Bright Eyes, have worked with or opened for Baker.

Along with original compositions, she has also recorded renditions of songs by Elliott Smith, Radiohead, and Soundgarden.

With Phoebe Bridgers and Lucy Dacus, two other independent singer-songwriters with whom she has previously performed, Baker created the rock supergroup Boygenius in 2018.

In August of that year, the trio released three songs. They then announced an eponymous EP, Boygenius, which was published on October 26, 2018, to resounding critical praise.

Baker released two 7-inch singles in 2019. The first, which was released in June, included the songs “Red Door” and “Conversation Piece,” while the second, which was released in October as part of the Sub Pop singles series, included the songs “Tokyo” and “Sucker Punch.”

The four songs were well praised and all had a slightly more polished sound than her earlier work.

On October 21, 2020, Baker released the lead single “Faith Healer” off her upcoming third studio album, Little Oblivions, along with an essay written by poet Hanif Abdurraqib.
