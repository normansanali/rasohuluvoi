Career

In Rochester, New York, Williams played basketball for University Prep Charter School for Young Men. He was chosen to the Class AA all-state fifth team as a junior after averaging 22 points, seven rebounds, and five assists per game. Williams moved to Prolific Prep in Napa, California, for his senior year, where he scored 15.8 points per game. On the Amateur Athletic Union circuit, he played for the City Rocks with Isaiah Stewart and Buddy Boeheim. Williams, a four-star prospect, chose Buffalo over Syracuse, Virginia, Pittsburgh, Temple, and St. Bonaventure to play collegiate basketball. He became the program’s highest-rated recruit in its history.

Freshman Williams averaged 3.2 points and 1.7 rebounds for Buffalo. In his second season, he started and averaged 11.6 points and 4.7 rebounds. He worked with Florida and California tutors to enhance his ballhandling and shooting after the season. On November 27, 2020, Williams scored 28 points and collected 12 rebounds in a 74-65 win against Towson. He was voted Second Team All-MAC as a junior after averaging 17.6 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 2.4 assists. Williams returned to Buffalo after withdrawing from the 2021 NBA draft. He scored a career-high 32 points and grabbed eight rebounds in his senior season debut against sixth-ranked Michigan on November 10, 2021. Williams earned MAC First Team.

Williams signed with the Portland Trail Blazers on April 1, 2023. The following day, he made his NBA debut, scoring 7 points in a 107-105 victory against the Minnesota Timberwolves. Williams was released by the Trail Blazers on July 28, 2023. Williams signed with the Houston Rockets on August 2, 2023.
