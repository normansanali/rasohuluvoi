Noah Seth Syndergaard, also known as “Thor,” is an American professional baseball pitcher.

He plays for the Los Angeles Dodgers in Major League Baseball (MLB). Syndergaard’s baseball journey flourished during his senior year at Mansfield Legacy High School in 2010.

A growth spurt and dedicated weight training routine significantly improved his pitch velocity, catching the attention of scouts.

Despite being overlooked by scouts and college coaches, he signed with the Toronto Blue Jays after being selected in the first round of the 2010 MLB Draft.

Syndergaard was later traded to the New York Mets, where he made his MLB debut in 2015.

He played a crucial role in the Mets’ journey to the 2015 World Series, pitching in their only win of the series.

Syndergaard has faced injury setbacks throughout his career, including Tommy John surgery in 2020.

In 2022, he signed with the Los Angeles Angels before being traded to the Philadelphia Phillies and then signing with the Dodgers for the 2023 season.

Noah Syndergaard’s Struggles Continue with Dodgers

Noah Syndergaard, a starting pitcher for the Los Angeles Dodgers, has been struggling this season.

He has a 6.12 ERA and a 1-3 record in seven starts.

Despite his struggles, there is a chance that Syndergaard will make his next start on Monday against the Minnesota Twins if his bullpen session goes well on Saturday.

However, fantasy managers may not be enthusiastic about having him in their lineups, given his performance this season.

During a recent game against the Tampa Bay Rays, Syndergaard allowed another six runs, causing his ERA to climb to 6.27.

This places him among the pitchers with the highest ERAs in the league for those with 50 innings or more.

The Dodgers had signed Syndergaard with the expectation that he could regain his dominant form and reclaim his 100mph fastball. Unfortunately, he has yet to achieve that goal.

Syndergaard expressed his frustration, stating that he feels like his last weapons have been taken away from him.

He acknowledged that his current repertoire is insufficient to compete against opposing teams effectively.

The Dodgers manager, Dave Roberts, recognized Syndergaard’s desire to regain his fastball velocity but also emphasized the importance of command.

While there have been discussions about potentially giving up on Syndergaard and moving on from the $13 million investment, the Dodgers currently lack a clear replacement option.

The team is thin on pitching staff due to injuries, making it challenging to find immediate alternatives.

However, with prospects performing well at the Double-A level, the Dodgers may consider calling them up if needed.
