Lucrezia Guidone On Netflix’s “Devotion, a Story of Love and Desire”

Netflix’s new Italian erotic drama “Devotion, a Story of Love and Desire” showcases the mental torment and pain that comes with the physical pleasure of infidelity. In the trailer, protagonist Carlo played by Michele Riondino asks, “To whom do we owe our fidelity? To others, or ourselves?”

And the actress playing his real estate agent wife Margherita is actress Lucrezia Guidone. They are a happily married couple from the outside looking in and have a strong connection behind closed doors. But, the opportunities to cheat have an affair arises for both spouses.

While Margherita is expected to cheat with a massage therapist, Andrea played by Leondardo Pazzagli whereas Carlo is expected to cheat with his mysterious student Sofia played by Carolina Sala. The show is directed by Andrea Molaioli and Stefano Cipani.

The show is based on Italian author Marco Missiroli’s bestselling 2019 novel Fedeltà, translation “Fidelity”. Speaking with Opentapes Lucrezia said, “After what we’ve experienced in the series, it better be kept quiet” when asked whether infidelity be confessed or should be kept hush-hush?

Lucrezia Guidone Net Worth

Lucrezia Guideone has built a sizable net worth of $750 thousand as an actress.

She trained professionally at Santa Cristina Theater Center directed by Luca Ronconi and Roberta Carlotto. Lucrezia also attended the Lee Strasberg Theater & Film Institute of New York. To further polish her acting skills she worked joined Susan Batson Studio of New York and Jordan Bayne NYC.

She is a graduate of Silvio D’Amico National Academy of Dramatic Art of Rome and also got the Preparatory acting course at the Experimental Center of Cinematography.

Her acting career started in theater and she first appeared on the production of “Noi non eroi” tratto da “Quattro bombe in tasca” by U.  Chiti. “Shakespea Re di Napoli”, “Se Amleto avesse potuto”, “L’Impresario delle canarie”, “Psicosi delle 4 e 48”, “Illusion Comique”, “Frammenti”, “In cerca d’ autore”, “Don Giovanni”, and “Antigone” are among her notable works.

Whereas, Lucrezia also worked on radio “La perfetta letizia” in 2008 and “Le città invisibili” in 2010.

Her on-screen works on movies and TV shows are Dov’è Mario?, Dove cadono le ombre, The Girl in the Fog, Senza Distanza, Luna Nera, Summertime, The King of Laughter, and Padri among others.

For her role on TV shows and theatres, she has received several awards. Her most recent awards were the “Young talent” Award from Repubblica, Spoleto Festival in 2017, and Best Actress at the Melbourne Indie FilmFestival, for the role of Mina in the film “Time Zone Inn” in 2016.
