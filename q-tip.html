Career

Q Tip is known for his intellectual, mystical, and introspective lyrical themes, as well as his creative jazz-influenced hip-hop production style. He began his musical career as an MC and major producer for the iconic alternative hip hop group A Tribe Called Quest in the late 1980s. He co-founded the production duo The Ummah in the mid-1990s, and his gold-certified solo debut Amplified was released in 1999. In the 2000s, he released The Renaissance, which was nominated for a Grammy Award, and Kamaal the Abstract, an experimental album.
Q-Tip has acted in a number of films, including Poetic Justice, She Hate Me, and Prison Song, the latter of which he co-wrote and played the lead role in.
